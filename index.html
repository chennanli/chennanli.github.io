From industrial RAG to a bounded LLM agent: a root-cause-analysis workbench

A read-only prototype on the Tennessee Eastman Process: from fixed-keyword RAG through a bounded agent workflow, with deterministic detection underneath and human review at the top.

An industrial root-cause-analysis prototype built on the published TEP simulator. Starts from a fixed-keyword RAG baseline with a live PCA/T² detector and a three-LLM panel, then moves to a single-ReAct agent over six read-only tools, then to a bounded LangGraph workflow with four parallel evidence layers, a critic step, and a human-review gate. The transferable patterns are the division of labor — deterministic detection underneath, read-only evidence tools around it, a bounded workflow above them, and a policy check before any advisory reaches a human reviewer.

Part 1: Can mandated industrial setbacks ease the AI infrastructure bottleneck?
